\subsection{Fundamental Ramsey Theorems}
\label{sec:1.2}

In this section we define $\RKG{s}$ as a red coloured complete graph on $s$
vertices and analogously, $\BKG{t}$ as a blue coloured complete graph on $t$
vertices.
We also write $\RKG{s} \subseteq K_{n}$ to denote that $\RKG{s}$ is a subgraph
of $K_n$ for any colouring of $K_n$.

\begin{definition}
  For $s,t \in \Z^+$, define $R(s,t)$ as the smallest $n \in \Z^+$ such that
  $\RKG{s}\subseteq K_n$ or $\BKG{s}\subseteq K_n$.
\end{definition}
